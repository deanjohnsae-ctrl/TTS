12 Indians among 13 killed in explosion at Qatar factory

A screen grab of the blast at a factory in Qatar. AFP

Dubai, PTI: Twelve Indians were among 13 people killed in an explosion at a factory in Qatar, the Ministry of External Affairs said on Wednesday.

The blast occurred at a plastics factory in the Mesaieed Industrial City, about 40 km south of Doha. Emergency services rushed to the scene after the explosion, which sent a massive fireball into the sky.

Qatar's Ministry of Interior confirmed the explosion occurred around 6:30 am local time. Initial investigations suggest a gas leak may have triggered the blast, though an official inquiry is underway.

The Indian Embassy in Doha is in contact with Qatari authorities to obtain more details about the victims and to provide consular assistance to their families.

Qatar has a large Indian expatriate population, numbering over 700,000, who work across various sectors including construction, healthcare, and hospitality.

The Mesaieed Industrial City is one of Qatar's key industrial zones and houses numerous petrochemical and manufacturing plants.

Families of the deceased are being notified, and the government has assured full support for repatriation of mortal remains.
